#1 avoid the major culprits
many popular fashion stores are responsible for fast fashion. it is important to note which stores are included in this mix so you are able to make a wise shopping trip.
#2 do your research
have you ever purchased something just because it was a good deal? well, did you ever stop to think about why the shirt was that cheap? look into the average cost to make a shirt so you can determine whether something was made unethically based on price.
#3 shop second hand
expand the lifecycle of an article of clothing and shop second hand. thrift stores are an excellent place to add something new to your wardrobe at an affordable price while being a sustainable shopper.
#4 love what you have
do you really need another t-shirt? most of the clothing purchases consumers make sit idle in their closet for days, months, or forever waiting to be worn or after they have been used once. love what you wear, and wear what you love.
#5 be a mindful consumer
it is important to sit back and think about whether or not you need another article of clothing. will you actually use it? don’t just buy things for the sake of buying things. a lot of work went into making it, don’t let it be for nothing.
#6 know the red flags
have you ever purchased something just because it was a good deal? well, did you ever stop to think about why the shirt was that cheap? look into the average cost to make a shirt so you can determine wether something was made unethically based on price.
